## Part 1: before running anything

1. An FFT describes a time-domain signal by showing which are its most important frequency sinusoidal components and how strong they are.
2. - The sampling rate or sampling frequence $f_s$ is the frequence used to scan the signal and is related to the Nyquist frequency through the Sampling Theorem:
   $f_s > 2 \cdot f_n.   $
     It tells us how many samples per second are captured from the signal.
   - The Nyquist frequency is the highest frequency that can be represented faithfully by a DFT.
   - The frequency resolution is the distance between the FFT frequence bins. Given a sampling frequency $f_s$ and a number $N$ of samples, the frequency resolution is defined as $\Delta f = \frac{f_s}{N} = \frac{1}{T},$
   with $T$ the total observation time.

3. The DFT/FFT formula assumes samples at equally-spaced times $t_i = i \cdot \Delta t$.
